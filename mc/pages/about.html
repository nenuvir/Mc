This is an exciting new project offering opportunities for both adults & youths to learn mechanical/practical/teamwork skills (and also co-drive or drive, for those who demonstrate long-term commitment).The vehicles are worked on in Bicester/Reading whenever people want to get together (normally a mix of week and weekend days, mainly during daylight hours). There are several qualified mechanics involved with the project who do ‘hands on’ training/mentoring of the other team members.Apart from being good craic with the other team members, it's a great way of building up skills/CV experience and networking.One of the lads has already landed a job working for one of the team's mechanics; another lad has got into the Territorial Army via a contact from the team.

The team often works in partnership with youth educational establishments/colleges. The rally activities are integrated with youth projects (which includes positive role modelling for those from disadvantaged/troubled backgrounds). The project is recognised by the Go4 community-based social enterprise as a 'voluntary-work placement' http://www.go4enterprises.org/ By joining this Facebook group you will get invites to the events (usually maintenance sessions on the Team’s vehicles or Events e.g. rallies; sometimes shows & local motorclub pub nights as well).

The sessions often last for the majority of the day (or possibly even until the light fades at dusk).Feel free to arrive later or leave earlier if you need to –but it is important that you let the rest of the team know when you are coming (by responding to the Facebook Event Invite) in case it is cancelled or rescheduled for any reason e.g. weather/parts not arrived/mechanic cancelled etc. When coming to sessions, you should bring: 1. A boiler suit or waterproofs (for dirty work lying on ground working on the cars). You can then take the dirty layer off if we go inside the house/sit down (without arousing the wrath of the wife!). Getting your clothes minging and then sitting in the rally car's seats isn't on. 2. Warm clothes. Thermal underwear and lots of layers+ hat & gloves. Rally servicing is the ‘mud & guts’ of motorsport & is usually done in a bleak, muddy car park. It’s always wet & cold; "the wetter the better - if it ain’t raining, it ain’t training” 3. Any food & drink to keep you going during the day (we can do hot drinks but I'm banned by the wife from taking over the kitchen etc. so I can't offer lunch any more I'm afraid) 4. Proof of address & proof of employer (if relevant) 5. Proof of any relevant qualifications you have 6. Photo ID (always bring this) 7. A sense of humour. Never leave home without it; you will need it to get you through the gritty times and make them good craic. If you are interested in getting involved (especially Adult volunteers and those with mechanical skills) or to refer an individual then message us with your contact details.
